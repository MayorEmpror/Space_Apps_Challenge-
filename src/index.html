Lunar Eclipse

Lunar eclipses are celestial events that occur when the Earth passes between the Sun and the Moon, causing the Earth's shadow to be cast on the Moon's surface. These events are fascinating astronomical phenomena and have been studied and recorded for centuries.

Observation

Lunar eclipses are safe to observe with the naked eye, unlike solar eclipses, which require protective eyewear.

Total Solar Eclipse

A total solar eclipse is a spectacular astronomical event that occurs when the Moon passes directly between the Earth and the Sun, temporarily blocking out the Sun's light The path of totality is the area on Earth where the total solar eclipse can be observed in its full glory. It is typically quite narrow, ranging from a few kilometers to a few hundred kilometers in width.

Observation

Observing a total solar eclipse requires special precautions.

Partial Solar Eclipse

A partial solar eclipse is a celestial event that occurs when only a portion of the Sun is obscured by the Moon as seen from a specific location on Earth. Unlike a total solar eclipse where the Sun is completely covered, during a partial solar eclipse, a fraction of the Sun remains visible.

Observation

It's essential to emphasize that observing any phase of a partial solar eclipse without proper eye protection can be harmful to the eyes.
